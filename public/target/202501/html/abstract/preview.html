초록 (Abstract)
Drinking water distribution system pipes should undergo biostable suitability tests before deployment. Small-diameter pipes were cut into pieces for testing, but this method is unsuitable for large-diameter, thick, or metal pipes as the cutting edges and outer surface can affect accuracy. This study optimized the experimental design by selecting biostable test water and using glass caps for large-diameter pipes. Polyvinyl chloride (PVC) and ductile iron (DI) pipes coated with cement or epoxy were investigated, and DI coated with cement had significant biomass formation potential. Biomass formation results were compared with other national standards, revealing that some selected materials may not be suitable. An advanced semi-automated biomass formation test was conducted for the first time, which can monitor bacterial growth behaviour and fingerprint changes in real-time using an online flow cytometry system.
